**Competition Aircraft Design**
  * Important discussion between VTOL or fixed-wing design
  * **VTOL**
    * David made a test small VTOL to learn Ardupilot VTOL features and get more info for our ultimate decision
    * Pros
      * less stressful takeoffs at Black Mtn
      * Higher payload capacity/more flight time
        * can make the plane wings smaller as they only need to support hover and not takeoff
      * increase time over target for test flights (hover)
    * Cons
      * Complex manufacturing
      * Complex operation
      * Cost - at max $1000 more for 4 motors
      * development time longer
    * Embedded does not need to change much other than supporting the extra power draw
    * Software should not need to change much (still using Arduplane) and Ardupilot has good q_assist failsafe parameters
    * Airdrop mechanism should not be affected by this decision
    * _Effect on batteries is unknown_ ; most time spent in cruising will still use same amount of energy
    * Discussion:
      * No major complaints from software and embedded leads
      * worst case fallback to onboarding plane
      * we can incrementally test VTOL hover to get reassurance this is working
      * stiffness is a concern, but toothless was very rigid
      * manufacturing is fundamentally the same. Difference is adding tail booms and motor mounting and unknown unknowns
    * Decision: slight preference among members for VTOL Guided payload
  * Thinking of using onshape
  * need collaboration among airframe and embedded


**Embedded**
  * Workshops done for: arduino, soldering, PCB design, mavlink
  * working on signals board onboarding project for onboarding plane
